The iMac G4 was released January of 2002 with a stunning design. I've heard it called, 'the lamp computer', 'the sunflower', 'the dome', etc.. In any case, its retro-furtist design attracted me immediately. As a dedicated internet/tech nostaglist, it's only natural that I'd own one of these hahah


I had very little experience working with computer hardware. Much less Apple "logic boards" from over two decades ago. This project was almost DOA three years ago unless I felt like learning about such things (which I didn't). That's when I found Juicy Crumb - a small group of engineers dedicated to giving a second life to old, gorgeous computers such as this one.

The process of replacing the logic boards was very easy, even for how clumsy I am. I have the 17 inch model specifically. Now, since the Juicy Crumb dock turns the Mac into a monitor, its optical drive does not work :C And I have lots of accessories plugged into the back of it haha.

Right now it has an M2 hooked up to it but I can realistically use any other apple device with it! I use it for casual browsing and making my silly little programs, but it can handle quite a lot! I'm so impressed by this tiny thing. Looking into how to run some AAA games on my iMac G4... I crave Oblivion. Was an expensive but totally worth it project. In the future I hope to do this with a G3, or possibly replace the insides completely and find a way to get a functional CD drive!
